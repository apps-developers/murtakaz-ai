# Performance Management Frameworks

## What Is a Performance Management Framework?

A **performance management framework** is a structured system that connects an organization's strategy to individual and team actions through defined goals, measures, review cycles, and accountability mechanisms.

Without a framework, performance management becomes ad hoc — different departments measure different things, reviews happen inconsistently, and leadership cannot compare performance across the organization.

---

## The Balanced Scorecard (BSC)

Developed by **Robert Kaplan and David Norton** at Harvard in the early 1990s, the Balanced Scorecard is the most widely adopted strategic management framework in the world.

### Core Idea
Traditional management focused almost entirely on **financial metrics** (profit, revenue, cost). The BSC argues that financial outcomes are **lagging indicators** — by the time you see them, it's too late to react. Organizations need a balanced view across four perspectives:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                       VISION & STRATEGY                  │
└──────────┬──────────────────────────────────┬───────────┘
           │                                  │
  ┌────────▼────────┐                ┌────────▼────────┐
  │   FINANCIAL     │                │   CUSTOMER      │
  │  How do we look │                │  How do our     │
  │  to shareholders│                │  customers      │
  │  and funders?   │                │  see us?        │
  └────────┬────────┘                └────────┬────────┘
           │                                  │
  ┌────────▼────────┐                ┌────────▼────────┐
  │ INTERNAL        │                │  LEARNING &     │
  │ PROCESSES       │                │  GROWTH         │
  │ What must we    │                │  Can we         │
  │ excel at?       │                │  improve and    │
  │                 │                │  create value?  │
  └─────────────────┘                └─────────────────┘
```

### BSC in Practice
Each perspective contains:
- **Objectives** — what the organization aims to achieve
- **Measures (KPIs)** — how progress is tracked
- **Targets** — the desired level of performance
- **Initiatives** — the actions that will drive results

## OKRs — Objectives and Key Results

Popularized by Intel and later Google, **OKRs** are a goal-setting framework that separates the *what* (Objective) from the *how we'll know* (Key Results).

### Structure

```
OBJECTIVE: "Build a world-class customer experience"
  └── Key Result 1: Achieve CSAT score ≥ 90% by Q4
  └── Key Result 2: Reduce average resolution time to < 4 hours
  └── Key Result 3: Increase NPS from 32 to 55
```

### OKR Principles
- **Ambitious**: OKRs should be stretch goals — achieving 70% is considered success
- **Transparent**: All OKRs are visible to everyone in the organization
- **Quarterly cadence**: OKRs are set and reviewed every quarter
- **Decoupled from compensation**: OKRs should not be tied directly to bonuses to avoid sandbagging

### OKR vs. KPI

| Dimension | KPI | OKR |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| **Purpose** | Monitor ongoing performance | Drive ambitious change |
| **Cadence** | Monthly/quarterly/yearly | Quarterly |
| **Target style** | Realistic (100% = success) | Stretch (70% = success) |
| **Visibility** | Often department-specific | Organization-wide |
| **Change rate** | Stable, long-lived | Reset each quarter |

OKRs and KPIs are **complementary** — KPIs track the health of the business; OKRs drive transformation.

## The EFQM Excellence Model

The **European Foundation for Quality Management (EFQM)** model is a quality and excellence framework widely used in the Middle East and Gulf region, including Saudi Arabia's government sector and Vision 2030-aligned organizations.

### Core Structure: Enablers + Results

```
ENABLERS (What an organization does)
  ├── Leadership
  ├── Strategy
  ├── People
  ├── Partnerships & Resources
  └── Processes, Products & Services
              │
              ▼
RESULTS (What an organization achieves)
  ├── Customer Results
  ├── People Results
  ├── Society Results
  └── Business Results
```

EFQM uses a **RADAR logic** (Results, Approaches, Deploy, Assess & Refine) for scoring, and organizations are scored out of 1,000 points.

## The Strategy Map

A **Strategy Map** (also from Kaplan & Norton) is a visual representation of the cause-and-effect relationships between strategic objectives across BSC perspectives.

```
FINANCIAL
  "Increase shareholder value"
        ↑ caused by
CUSTOMER
  "Retain existing customers"  +  "Attract new customers"
        ↑ caused by
INTERNAL PROCESS
  "Deliver on-time" + "Improve service quality"
        ↑ caused by
LEARNING & GROWTH
  "Train staff" + "Implement new CRM system"
```

Strategy Maps make the logic of the strategy explicit — if we improve X, it will drive Y, which will drive Z. This is the theoretical basis for **linked KPIs and weighted scores** in platforms like Rafed KPI.

## Hoshin Kanri (Policy Deployment)

**Hoshin Kanri** is a Japanese strategic planning methodology that ensures the entire organization is aligned around a small number of breakthrough objectives.

### The X-Matrix
The central tool is the **X-Matrix**, which maps:
- Breakthrough objectives (long-term)
- Annual priorities (yearly targets)
- Improvement initiatives (projects)
- Performance metrics (KPIs)
- Accountable teams

### Catchball
A key Hoshin concept is **catchball** — goals are not just handed down top-to-bottom. They are "thrown" from leadership to the next level, who "catch" them, negotiate feasibility, and "throw back" their response. This creates genuine two-way alignment.

## Saudi Arabia's Context: Vision 2030 and National Performance Management

Saudi Arabia has developed one of the world's most ambitious national performance management systems as part of **Vision 2030**.

### Vision Realization Programs (VRPs)
Vision 2030 is operationalized through **Vision Realization Programs (VRPs)** — each with defined:
- Objectives linked to Vision pillars
- KPIs with national targets
- Responsible entities
- Quarterly reporting cadence

### National KPI Culture
This has created a strong **KPI culture** in Saudi public and private sector organizations:
- Organizations are expected to report performance data regularly
- KPIs are tied to organizational and leadership evaluation
- Digital dashboards and performance systems are now standard practice

Rafed KPI is designed specifically to serve this context — enabling Saudi organizations to implement structured, governed, bilingual (Arabic/English) performance management aligned with Vision 2030 principles.

## Choosing the Right Framework

| Organization Type | Recommended Framework |
|------------------|-----------------------|
| Government / Public Sector | EFQM, Balanced Scorecard, Vision 2030 alignment |
| Large Private Enterprise | Balanced Scorecard, Strategy Maps |
| Technology / Startups | OKRs |
| Manufacturing / Operations | Hoshin Kanri, Lean KPIs |
| Hybrid (most Saudi orgs) | BSC + KPIs with OKR-style ambition |

The frameworks are not mutually exclusive. Most mature organizations blend elements from multiple frameworks to suit their culture and context.
